Data Breach Scenarios

Real-world examples of phishing attacks and their consequences

  • 1. John Podesta’s Email (2016)

    One of the Biggest Data Breaches from Phishing.

    There was a lot of controversy surrounding the November 2016 election. One of the most notable was the hack of John Podesta’s Gmail account. Podesta, chairman of Hillary Clinton’s campaign, was victimized by a Russian hacker group known as Fancy Bear. The phishers, pretending to be Google, sent an email claiming he needed to change his password due to an attempted hack. The email linked to a malicious website, giving hackers access to his account and leading to the release of thousands of emails via WikiLeaks.

  • 2. BenefitMall (2018)

    Human Resource & Payroll Data Exposure.

    BenefitMall was affected by a phishing attack between June and October 2018. The company’s website was accessed via compromised employee email credentials. Exposed information potentially included:

    Names
    Email addresses
    Birth dates
    Bank account information
    Insurance premium info

  • 3. Sony Pictures (2014)

    State-Sponsored Cyberattack.

    In retaliation for the movie “The Interview,” a North Korean government-backed group launched a devastating attack. Using phishing and spear-phishing emails containing malware, attackers gained access to Sony’s network, performed covert reconnaissance, stole confidential data, and disabled thousands of computers. The attack is thought to have cost the company upwards of $100 million.

  • 4. Methodist Hospitals (2019)

    Healthcare Data Compromise.

    A phishing attack compromised more than 68,000 patients’ information. Two email accounts were compromised, exposing names, addresses, health insurance info, Social Security numbers, bank account numbers, and treatment information.

  • 5. University of Wisconsin-Parkside (2019)

    Financial Fraud via Routing Numbers.

    A phishing attack prompted an employee to change the routing numbers of two vendors. Before the issue was discovered, the university lost $315,000 in fraudulent bank transfers.
